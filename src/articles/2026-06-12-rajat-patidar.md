On May 31 in Ahmedabad, Rajat Patidar lifted the Indian Premier League trophy for the second year running. His Royal Challengers Bengaluru had beaten Gujarat Titans by five wickets in the final. The statistics from across the season confirmed what the result made official: **501 runs in 16 matches at a strike rate of 192.36**. Only two captains before him in IPL history — MS Dhoni and Rohit Sharma — had led their side to the title in successive years.

Patidar is from Indore. He grew up in a city that has produced capable cricketers for decades, played his domestic seasons quietly for Madhya Pradesh, and graduated into the national conversation through a single innings. In the IPL 2022 Eliminator, he walked out against Lucknow Super Giants as a replacement for a concussed teammate — uncapped by India at the time — and scored **112 not out off 54 balls**, the first century by an uncapped player in IPL playoff history. He struck 12 boundaries and seven sixes and put RCB to 207/4.

That same year, he was the leading run-scorer for Madhya Pradesh as they won the Ranji Trophy for the first time in the state's history. In the final against Mumbai, he made 122 to give MP a 162-run first-innings lead. They won by six wickets.

## A Season Without Gaps

In the 2026 IPL, Patidar did not leave spaces for the competition. The final itself was managed rather than scrambled: RCB held GT to 155/8, then Virat Kohli's 75 off 42 balls settled the chase with two overs to spare. Patidar's batting across the season provided the consistent top-order foundation that gave RCB the margin they needed game after game.

After the match, he kept to his pattern of plain statement. "I think we have not just played, we dominated this year," he told reporters. On his style of leadership — noted throughout the season for its quiet, tactical focus rather than public intensity — he said: "My way of captaincy is different. I am not that expressive, but at the same time I am aware of the game situation."

Royal Challengers Bengaluru became only the third franchise in IPL history to successfully defend the title, joining Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings.

In June 2026, Patidar is back in Madhya Pradesh. He is captaining Gwalior Cheetahs in the MPL T20 Scindia Cup — the state franchise tournament, now in its third year — having won the biggest prize in Indian domestic cricket and then chosen to come home and play the next one.

## What It Means for Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh has built strong domestic cricketers for a long time without the national headlines to match. Patidar changed that in two distinct moments: the 2022 Ranji Trophy, which belonged to the whole squad, and the two IPL titles, which carried his name as captain. He joins Dhoni and Rohit Sharma in a record that will not grow quickly. The space between them and everyone else in IPL captaincy history is, for now, the width of one consecutive title.

He is 33, still playing, and he is playing in Indore.
