On 26 June 2026 at the Holkar Stadium in Indore, Chambal Ghariyals beat Royal Nimar Eagles by **31 runs** to win the MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026. Their captain, Shubham Sharma, had scored **57** and helped put up **224 for 5** — enough to end the tournament with a record nobody in the competition had managed to breach. Not one opponent beat Chambal Ghariyals across the entire tournament.

It is a rare thing in cricket for a team to go through a full event without losing. It is rarer still for both the men's and women's sides of the same franchise to do it in the same edition. Chambal Ghariyals' women's team had already lifted the title unbeaten earlier in the tournament. When the men's final ended with the scoreboard in their favour at Holkar Stadium, it completed a **historic double** in Madhya Pradesh cricket.

The third-wicket partnership of **83 runs** between Shubham Sharma and Apurve Dwivedi was the innings that turned the final. The pair combined with the match at a critical stage, shifting the momentum decisively. Royal Nimar Eagles, chasing 225, were dismissed for 193.

## A Career Built on Patience

Shubham Sharma made his first-class debut in November 2013 against Baroda at Vadodara. He has been playing red-ball cricket for Madhya Pradesh for nearly 12 years. He was among the probables for India's Under-19 World Cup squad in 2012 but was not selected in the final group. He has never played in the IPL. He went nowhere fancy. He kept turning up for Madhya Pradesh.

The returns on that loyalty are substantial. Since the 2021-22 domestic season, Shubham has accumulated more first-class runs — **2,849** — than any other batter who has played at least 40 innings across the top-flight domestic competitions in India, at an average of **52.75** with **nine centuries**. In MP's maiden Ranji Trophy win in 2021-22, he scored **608 runs** in six matches, including four hundreds and a fifty; his **116 in the final against Mumbai** set up a six-wicket victory.

His batting approach is described by those who cover domestic cricket as grinding rather than spectacular — he leans into his shots, drives the ball late, and builds his innings through accumulated singles and well-timed boundaries. In T20 he applies the same discipline: score what the team needs, stay until it is enough. At MPL 2026 that produced 57 in the final, 81 in a group-stage game, and a clean sheet across every match he captained.

> "Winning the title feels even more special after watching our women's team achieve the same earlier in the tournament. Their success gave us confidence and motivation. Throughout the competition, our biggest strength was consistency. Different players stepped up in different situations, and that collective effort made all the difference."

For Madhya Pradesh cricket, Shubham Sharma represents something genuinely valuable: a player who spent over a decade grinding through the domestic circuit, building a team, and winning. He is not a celebrity outside cricket circles. He does not carry a franchise auction price tag. What he carries instead is a record that puts him among the finest domestic batters in the country, and now a tournament sweep — men's and women's both — to complete the story.
