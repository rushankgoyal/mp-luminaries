When the judges at the Miss Universe Madhya Pradesh 2026 state audition in Bhopal announced her name, Harshita Sahu had just beaten contestants gathered from every corner of the state. The 23-year-old from Ruthiyai — a railway junction town in Guna district in the Chambal region — will now represent Madhya Pradesh at Miss Universe India 2026, the national finals scheduled to run from July 19 to August 23, with the grand finale in New Delhi.

The selection panel included Nikhil Anand, owner of Miss Universe India; Manika Vishwakarma, the 2025 title-holder and one of India's top models; and Amjad Khan, the organisation's director. Their criteria reach beyond presentation: leadership, community engagement, and the clarity with which a contestant articulates her purpose. Sahu cleared each bar.

A Computer Science graduate, she works as a Data Analyst at Deutsche Bank — a career built on precision and inference, not on the entertainment circuit. Outside office hours, she has volunteered with HelpAge India on issues facing the elderly, with the Muskurahat Foundation supporting educational welfare for underprivileged children, and with UNI, the United News of India. When asked what drives her, she was direct:

> "For youth from small towns, no dream is too big if pursued with courage and persistent effort."

## From Ruthiyai to the National Stage

Ruthiyai is not a city anyone discusses when they talk about India's talent pipelines. Guna district, where it sits, is better known for agriculture and the flat plains of the Chambal region. Yet the Miss Universe India system has a history of surfacing exactly this kind of story — and Harshita Sahu makes the case powerfully.

Dainik Jagran, reporting on her selection, traced a lineage that stretches from Sushmita Sen's Miss Universe win in 1994 through Lara Dutta's 2000 title, both figures who had connections to the state. Sahu is now the latest name in that line — though the 2026 edition has its own scale: **124 national finalists**, competing across disciplines before a grand finale in August.

She did not move to a larger city to build this platform. She built it from Guna: with a technical degree, a professional job, and a years-long record of giving time to causes that don't involve a spotlight. That combination — the data analyst who also spends evenings at an old-age welfare organisation — is precisely what made the panel take notice.

Whether she returns with a crown or not, Harshita Sahu has already done the harder thing: she arrived at that Bhopal audition from a place most contestants don't come from, she stood in a field of the state's most prepared young women, and the three judges chose her. Guna district will be watching New Delhi closely in August.
