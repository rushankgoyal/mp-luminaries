On June 13, the MPL T20 Scindia Cup women's final takes place at Holkar Cricket Stadium in Indore. Pooja Vastrakar will be on the field, captaining Chambal Ghariyals. That she is there reflects the quality of her cricket this month. How she got there reflects something with a longer arc.

Vastrakar was born in Shahdol, Madhya Pradesh, in 1999. She learned the game playing with boys in her neighbourhood, found her identity as a medium-fast bowler under coach Ashutosh Shrivastava, and then at 16 suffered a torn ACL — a career-threatening injury that ended many young players' journeys at the same age. She came back. She made her India debut in February 2018 against South Africa. In June 2021 she walked out to play her first Test match, against England at Bristol, and took **5 wickets for 93 runs** in the innings.

That five-wicket haul is one data point in a career that now runs to 72 T20 internationals, 33 ODIs and five Tests. Across all three formats, the combined bowling column reads **100 international wickets**. The combined batting column reads **1,028 runs**.

## Chambal Ghariyals, Unbeaten

In the 2026 MPL Scindia Cup women's competition — the third edition of the Madhya Pradesh state franchise tournament, this year expanded and played in Indore — Vastrakar has been the captain Chambal Ghariyals needed. The team went through the league phase without a loss. In one match, Vastrakar scored **63 off 35 balls**, a knock that set the tone for a total the opposition could not chase. Her medium-pace bowling has taken wickets at crucial moments throughout.

Her approach to the final has been direct. "We had discussed right from the beginning that there would be many distractions, so we would cut them off and focus only on bat and ball," she told reporters in June 2026. "We try not to take the extra pressure that comes with a final. Instead, we see pressure as a privilege and treat every game as an opportunity."

She has also been speaking publicly about the India women's team, currently at the T20 World Cup in England — a squad she is not part of for this tournament, though she has been clear about her focus on returning. Her message to the team: "They did very well in the ODI World Cup, so my message would be to just continue that in the T20 World Cup as well. Anyone among them who handles the pressure will be the hero of this World Cup." India won the Women's ODI World Cup in 2025 under Harmanpreet Kaur.

## Shahdol to Holkar

The Holkar Cricket Stadium in Indore is not close to Shahdol. Shahdol is a small district in the hills of eastern Madhya Pradesh; Indore is the state's commercial capital, more than 500 kilometres away. Vastrakar has spent her career crossing distances — from a small city to an international career, from an ACL surgery to a five-wicket Test debut, from the sidelines to the captaincy.

The MPL Scindia Cup is three years old. Vastrakar's presence as captain of a team in the final gives the women's edition a standard-bearer who can show young cricketers across the state what their own game might grow into. The path is visible because she walked it.
