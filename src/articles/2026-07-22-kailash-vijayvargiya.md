On July 2, 2026, at the 29th National Conference on e-Governance in Jaipur, Madhya Pradesh's Urban Development and Housing Department received the Silver Award for Cyber Security — one of 17 national recognitions across ten gold, six silver and one jury prize. Union Minister of State Dr Jitendra Singh presented the award, which went to the state's e-Nagar Palika portal: a single digital platform that links every municipality and corporation in Madhya Pradesh and delivers over 24 citizen services and 20 administrative modules online.

Kailash Vijayvargiya, Cabinet Minister for Urban Development and Housing since December 2023, has driven the department's digital reform agenda. He made clear what he sees the award as reflecting.

"Under the leadership of Chief Minister Dr Mohan Yadav, Madhya Pradesh has made unprecedented innovations in digital good governance, e-governance, cyber security and citizen services," Vijayvargiya said. "The e-Nagarpalika project has been developed with the objective of making citizen services simple, fast and transparent while ensuring the highest level of security for citizens' data."

## A Platform Built on Zero Trust

The cyber security recognition reflects a deliberate technical design that the department built in from the start. The e-Nagar Palika portal runs on modern cloud infrastructure and uses zero trust architecture — a model that requires continuous verification at every access point, rather than assuming a user can be trusted throughout their session once logged in. The platform also applies data encryption, continuous cyber monitoring and advanced security standards across all its services.

That architecture matters because of the scale at which it operates. Property tax payments, trade licence applications, birth and death registrations, and dozens of other municipal services run through it. The personal and financial data of millions of citizens moves through the system every day, across a state whose urban local bodies range from the million-population cities of Indore and Bhopal to smaller district municipalities where staff capacity is limited and the consequences of a data breach fall hardest on people with the fewest alternatives.

Seventeen initiatives from across India were honoured at the Jaipur ceremony. Being selected among the six Silver Award recipients placed MP's urban platform among the most technically secure citizen services in the country — a category that rewards depth of engineering rather than headline functionality.

That recognition carries weight beyond the certificate. Building a unified, secure urban portal for a state as varied as Madhya Pradesh — with the administrative breadth of its municipality network — is structurally more demanding than doing the same for a smaller or wealthier state. The e-Nagar Palika system was designed to function across that entire range. Its national recognition for cyber security, the hardest of the technical categories to win, reflects the seriousness with which the Urban Development Department has treated the infrastructure question under Vijayvargiya's watch.

For residents using the portal to pay a tax bill or submit a grievance, the award is invisible. The security it recognises is what makes the platform worth trusting.
