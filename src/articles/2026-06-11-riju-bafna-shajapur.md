Some collectors are remembered for the events they inaugurated. Riju Bafna seems determined to be remembered for something harder to photograph: water that stayed in the ground.

Shajapur, the district she has run since January 2024, is officially water-deficit — the kind of place where every summer is a slow-motion emergency. Rather than treat that as background fact, the **2014-batch IAS officer** has made water conservation the centrepiece of her administration. Under her watch, the district teamed up with the Madhya Pradesh Jan Abhiyan Parishad on a village-level campaign that is almost defiantly low-tech: sack-tying bunds to slow runoff, plugging drain water before it escapes, compacting soil, and turning bridges and culverts into small water-harvesting structures — with school students doing much of the work, and learning why it matters as they do it.

## More than one pipe dream

What makes Bafna's Shajapur agenda notable is its breadth. Alongside water, she has pushed two other quiet priorities: **maternal health services** — getting expectant mothers into the health system early and reliably — and **economic empowerment for rural women and self-help groups**, so that household incomes do not depend on a single monsoon. Agriculture and education round out the list. None of it is flashy; all of it compounds.

Bafna came to Shajapur after serving as collector of Narsinghpur, and she carries a reputation built across postings: an officer who picks a handful of measurable problems and grinds at them. In a state where collectors rotate fast and initiatives often fade with the transfer order, the discipline of sticking to fundamentals — water, mothers, livelihoods — is its own kind of leadership.

Madhya Pradesh's water-stressed districts will not be rescued by a single campaign season. But if Shajapur's drains, ponds and culverts hold a little more of this year's rain than last year's, thousands of farming families will feel the difference — and they will have a young collector's unfashionable priorities to thank for it.
