Two new solar parks came online in Madhya Pradesh on 29 June 2026. The 500 MW Neemuch Solar Park and the 450 MW Shajapur Solar Park were inaugurated together — a combined **950 MW** of new clean power capacity added to the state grid in a single day. Rakesh Shukla, Madhya Pradesh's Cabinet Minister for New and Renewable Energy, stood alongside Chief Minister Dr. Mohan Yadav and Union Minister Pralhad Joshi as the parks were formally opened. On the same occasion, the state signed a Power Purchase Agreement for a third project: the **440 MW Morena Solar Plus Storage Project**, the first solar-with-storage venture of its kind in the state.

The tariffs achieved across all three deals are what make the day stand out. The Neemuch units were secured at **₹2.14–₹2.15 per unit** — among the lowest ever recorded for ground-mounted solar in India. The Shajapur units ranged from **₹2.33 to ₹2.35 per unit**. The Morena storage project locked in at **₹2.70 per unit**, the lowest tariff recorded nationally for solar power bundled with battery storage. At that rate, MP's discoms and industrial consumers will eventually be able to buy solar power around the clock, not just during daylight hours, at a cost lower than coal-based generation from most existing plants in the country.

Shukla is an MLA from Mehgaon in Bhind district — his third stint in the Madhya Pradesh assembly, having first entered in 1998 and served again in 2008. When Chief Minister Dr. Mohan Yadav formed his cabinet in late 2023, Shukla took charge of the New and Renewable Energy portfolio. Over the roughly two and a half years since, the ministry has put out a sequence of competitive tenders — covering ground-mounted solar, feeder solarisation, floating solar, and now storage — that have attracted major developers and progressively driven tariffs down.

## What the Numbers Add Up To

Madhya Pradesh's total installed renewable energy capacity stood at approximately **10,366 MW** by early 2025, up from under 1,000 MW a decade earlier. The state has an ambitious target of becoming a leading renewable power producer — one that requires it to add capacity quickly and at rates that do not burden agriculture or household consumers who depend on subsidised power.

At ₹2.14 per unit, the Neemuch Solar Park costs less to generate from than coal at most legacy plants. That gap matters directly for the state's electricity bill. MP is one of India's larger agricultural power users, and farm power is subsidised from general state revenue; cheaper generation reduces that subsidy burden. The **₹1,593.98 crore** in projects inaugurated or laid on 29 June 2026 represent a single day's worth of that longer effort.

The Morena storage deal adds a capability that most Indian states do not yet have in operation at this scale: assured flat-block renewable power, available even after dark, at a tariff competitive with fossil fuel generation. That is a commercially meaningful step, not an incremental one.

For MP, the day's inaugurations are part of a steady policy push, and for Rakesh Shukla, they mark the clearest single demonstration yet of what his ministry has been building toward: a solar programme whose size and pricing put the state on the national map of renewable energy.
