The Viksit Bharat–Guarantee for Employment and Livelihood Mission (Rural) — known as VB-G RAM-G — came into national force on 1 July 2026. By the very next day, Madhya Pradesh was already moving. At a state-level event in Jabalpur, Prahlad Singh Patel, the Cabinet Minister for Panchayat and Rural Development, formally launched the programme for every district in the state, setting in motion what he called a new compact between government and the rural worker.

The speed was deliberate. Rather than wait for instructions to filter down the administrative chain, Patel had the state-level launch organised within 24 hours of the national rollout, with parallel events held across all 55 districts the same day. It is the kind of administrative urgency that turns a national promise from a gazette notification into a gram panchayat notice board.

## What 125 Days Actually Means

The VB-G RAM-G Act 2025 replaces the two-decade-old Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) with a broader mandate. Where MGNREGA guaranteed **100 days** of wage employment per rural household per year, VB-G RAM-G raises that floor to **125 days** — a **25 per cent increase**. Wages are indexed to inflation and paid directly into bank accounts, a design that removes the intermediary delays that slowed payments under the old system.

The mission is also built around digital implementation: job cards are issued digitally, work sites are geo-tagged, and attendance data is verified at the panchayat level before payments are triggered. It also explicitly includes livelihood support beyond daily wage work — covering household members who cannot participate in public works sites due to age, disability or caregiving responsibilities.

> "This mission is a unique blend of technology and transparency, which will strengthen good governance and encourage public participation in nation-building," Patel said at the Jabalpur launch. "It will generate employment, improve livelihoods and create sustainable community assets in rural areas."

Madhya Pradesh has more than 22,000 gram panchayats and a large rural population that historically depended on MGNREGA as a safety net during agricultural off-seasons. The additional 25 days under VB-G RAM-G are not just a statistical improvement — they represent a meaningful extension of the income window for households where every wage-day matters.

Patel brings direct experience to this role. Before returning to the MP state cabinet in December 2023 — winning a seat in the legislative assembly from Narsinghpur constituency — he served as Union Minister of State for Jal Shakti and Food Processing Industries in the Government of India. Both portfolios involved large-scale rural infrastructure and agricultural value-chain programmes. He resigned his Lok Sabha seat to take the state cabinet post, a choice that put him close to the ground-level machinery of village-level governance.

The transition from MGNREGA to VB-G RAM-G is the single largest structural shift in India's rural employment guarantee framework since the original law was passed in 2005. What Prahlad Singh Patel organised in Jabalpur on 2 July is the moment that shift stopped being national policy and became a reality in Madhya Pradesh.
