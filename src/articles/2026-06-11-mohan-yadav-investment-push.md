For decades, Madhya Pradesh sat in the middle of India's map but on the margins of its investment conversation. That is changing — and much of the change traces back to the patient, salesman-like persistence of Chief Minister Dr. Mohan Yadav.

In early June, the Chief Minister stood before delegates at the India–Latin America and Caribbean Trade and Investment Forum in Indore and made a case few would have made for the state a decade ago: that Madhya Pradesh belongs in conversations about global supply chains. He pointed to opportunities for collaboration in agriculture, food processing, pharmaceuticals, IT, energy, logistics and tourism, framing the state as "playing a pivotal role in the Prime Minister's mission to transform India into a prosperous global economic power."

It was not just talk. Weeks earlier, Dr. Yadav laid the foundation stone for the **₹2,360-crore Indore–Pithampur Economic Corridor**, a project designed to fuse the state's commercial capital with its industrial belt and transform Indore into a Central India growth hub. And behind these headline moments sits quieter, structural work: his government has rolled out **26 sector-specific industrial policies**, including first-of-their-kind state frameworks for space technology, artificial intelligence and renewable energy.

## The numbers behind the pitch

The results of this approach showed up at scale at the Global Investors Summit, which drew investment proposals worth over ₹32 lakh crore — of which roughly ₹8.57 lakh crore has already begun materialising on the ground. For a state long stereotyped as agrarian, those are numbers that change a reputation.

What distinguishes Dr. Yadav's economic push is its insistence that growth reach ordinary households. At the Indore forum, he returned repeatedly to a single theme: employment. Trade and investment, in his telling, are not ends in themselves but engines for jobs — for the welder in Pithampur, the logistics operator in Dewas, the software graduate in Bhopal who no longer needs to leave home to build a career.

A medical doctor by training and a wrestler's son from Ujjain, Dr. Yadav took office in December 2023 as something of a surprise choice. Two and a half years on, the surprise has worn off and a pattern has emerged: fewer spectacles, more foundation stones. Madhya Pradesh's rise on India's investment map is not an accident. It is being built, policy by policy, corridor by corridor.
