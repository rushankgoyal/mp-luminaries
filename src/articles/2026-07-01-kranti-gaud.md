On 30 June 2026 — one day after India were knocked out of the ICC Women's T20 World Cup in England — the BCCI announced the 15-member women's cricket squad for the Asian Games 2026 in Aichi-Nagoya, Japan. One name on that list: Kranti Gaud, a right-arm medium-fast bowler from Ghuwara village in Chhatarpur district, Madhya Pradesh.

India go to Aichi-Nagoya as defending champions. Cricket made its Asian Games debut at Hangzhou in 2023 and India won the gold. The squad that the BCCI named on 30 June, captained by Harmanpreet Kaur with Smriti Mandhana as vice-captain, includes Kranti Gaud as one of three specialist pace bowlers. The selectors' decision to retain her, even as India exited the T20 World Cup at the group stage, signals confidence in her continued role in the national pace attack.

## From Chhatarpur to a World Cup Medal in Under a Year

Kranti Gaud was born on 11 August 2003 in Ghuwara village, Chhatarpur district — a town in the Bundelkhand region of Madhya Pradesh, about halfway between Bhopal and Varanasi, better known for the Khajuraho temple complex nearby than for producing international cricketers. She represents Madhya Pradesh in the domestic circuit and made her national case by leading the state bowling attack to the Senior Women's One Day Trophy, taking **4 wickets for 25 runs** in the final.

Her international debut came in the Tri-Nation Series against Sri Lanka in May 2025. On India's subsequent tour of England, she took **6 wickets for 52 runs at Chester-le-Street**, becoming the youngest Indian woman to take a five-wicket haul in ODIs. That spell announced her clearly.

By the end of 2025, Gaud was a World Cup winner. She was part of the Indian squad that lifted the **2025 Women's Cricket World Cup**. From ODI debut to World Cup medal took less than eight months. In the WPL she plays for UP Warriorz, bringing her into franchise cricket alongside senior internationals every season.

At the ICC Women's T20 World Cup 2026 in England, Gaud was in India's 15-member group for the full tournament. The Asian Games squad selection on June 30 extends that run into the next event on the calendar.

Chhatarpur was not a district with a tradition of sending players to the Indian women's team. The Bundelkhand plateau is rural and agricultural; access to quality cricket infrastructure is not automatic there. A fast bowler from Ghuwara making it into a gold-medal defence squad is the newest entry in what has been a quiet, determined decade of growth for women's cricket in Madhya Pradesh.
