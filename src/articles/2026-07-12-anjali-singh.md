On 10 July 2026, two women boxers from the Madhya Pradesh State Boxing Academy boarded a flight for Glasgow, Scotland. Anjali Singh, competing in the **57 kg** category, and Mahi Lama, in the **60 kg** category, had been selected for the Commonwealth Boxing Camp — a two-week international training programme running through 24 July. Their selection drew a public congratulation from Madhya Pradesh's Sports and Youth Welfare Minister Vishvas Kailash Sarang, who called the opportunity an important milestone in both athletes' careers.

The Commonwealth Boxing Camp brings together elite boxers from across Commonwealth nations to train under world-class coaches. Participants take part in advanced fitness sessions, structured technical coaching, and sparring bouts with athletes from other countries. For a boxer still building her international profile, training in an environment like this offers exposure that domestic tournaments simply cannot provide: opponents with different styles, coaches from traditions outside India, and the kind of competitive pressure that forces adjustments.

Anjali Singh trains full-time at the Madhya Pradesh State Boxing Academy, the state government's dedicated facility for developing boxers at the national and international level. The academy competes at senior and junior national championships, and selections for international programmes like this camp signal that an athlete has reached the tier where the next meaningful test lies beyond India's borders.

## What Glasgow Offers

Being named for a Commonwealth training programme is not automatic. Selection requires athletes to have demonstrated consistent performance at the national level before the Indian Amateur Boxing Federation includes their names in an international programme. For Anjali Singh, the Glasgow camp opens several doors at once: access to superior technical coaching, high-quality sparring with Commonwealth-level competition, and the chance to begin building an international network that will matter throughout her career.

Women's boxing in India has gathered significant momentum in the past decade, with athletes from state academies regularly breaking through to Commonwealth Games podiums, Asian Championships, and World Championships. The pipeline that produces those results runs through exactly this kind of international exposure — arriving at foreign training camps, absorbing different methods, and returning home a sharper boxer.

Madhya Pradesh's Sports Minister said the Glasgow opportunity would play an important role in Anjali Singh's sporting journey. Two weeks of training at Commonwealth level, followed by the application of those lessons at home, is precisely how careers at the highest level are built — one intensive stint at a time.
