On the evening of 19 June 2026, at the conclusion of the three-day FATF Plenary in Paris, delegates from 39 member countries confirmed a new Vice-President for the 2026–27 term. The officer elected was **Vivek Aggarwal**, a 1994-batch IAS officer from the Madhya Pradesh cadre, currently serving as Secretary in India's Ministry of Culture. He also holds additional charge of the Ministry of Tourism.

The appointment is historic. India joined the Financial Action Task Force in 2010, and in the sixteen years since, it had never held the Vice-Presidency. That changes with Aggarwal's election.

The FATF is the international body that sets the standards by which countries combat money laundering, the financing of terrorism, and the financing of weapons proliferation. Its 40 Recommendations—regularly updated—form the basis of financial compliance law in more than 200 jurisdictions. Countries that fall short of FATF standards can be placed on its grey list or, in severe cases, its black list; either status carries real economic consequences, from tighter correspondent banking to reduced foreign investment. India itself has navigated FATF evaluations carefully. Having one of its officers now co-lead the body's governance is a significant strategic position.

## Three Decades Across Finance, Agriculture, and Culture

Aggarwal's career has moved across some of the most demanding assignments in Indian public administration. As Additional Secretary in the Department of Revenue, Ministry of Finance, he simultaneously served as Director of the **Financial Intelligence Unit–India (FIU-IND)**—the national body responsible for collecting, analysing, and disseminating reports of suspicious financial transactions from banks, financial institutions, and intermediaries. That role gave him direct and extended exposure to FATF's working processes and India's position within them.

Before the Finance Ministry, Aggarwal spent years in the Ministry of Agriculture, leading implementation of major national programmes: **PM-KISAN**, the digital agriculture mission, and the **Agriculture Infrastructure Fund**. These schemes together reach tens of millions of farming households across India. He holds a law degree and a commerce degree, both completed with first-division marks.

His transfer to the Ministry of Culture in April 2025 added yet another dimension: leading the ministry responsible for India's monuments, museums, and national heritage institutions.

## What the Post Requires

As FATF Vice-President, Aggarwal will work alongside the FATF President to coordinate inter-governmental work, oversee mutual evaluations of member countries' financial systems, and advance FATF's technical assistance to nations building out their compliance frameworks. The term runs from July 2026 to June 2027.

For the Madhya Pradesh cadre—one of India's larger administrative services, spread across governance roles from district collectorate to national ministries—this election is a mark of distinction. The cadre now has one of its own in a leadership chair at the institution that defines how the world's governments fight financial crime.
