On 10 July 2026, **Mahi Lama** boarded a flight from Bhopal to Glasgow, Scotland, as one of two women boxers selected from Madhya Pradesh for the Commonwealth Boxing Camp. At 23, the move was already her second international trip in a single season. Weeks earlier, she had been named to the Indian team for the Boxing World Cup 2026 in Guiyang, China — a squad that included world number-one-ranked Minakshi and some of the country's strongest women boxers.

Two international selections in one year from a state academy marks a young boxer as someone the selectors are watching closely.

Lama trains at the Madhya Pradesh State Boxing Academy in Bhopal, competing in the **60 kg** category. The Boxing World Cup, held in Guiyang in June 2026, was the first stage of major international competition. Her inclusion in India's squad for that event confirmed a standing in domestic boxing that is not reached without consistent national-level results.

The Commonwealth Boxing Camp, running from 10 to 24 July in Glasgow, is a different kind of opportunity. It is not competition — it is two weeks of structured training alongside elite boxers from Commonwealth nations, under international coaches, with sparring that presents styles and techniques that domestic tournaments rarely reproduce. Participants return having absorbed methods from boxing traditions outside India, and the adjustments that follow those sessions often show up in results months later.

## The Academy and What It Produces

The Madhya Pradesh State Boxing Academy, based in Bhopal, is the state government's dedicated facility for producing competitive boxers at the national and international level. The pathway it offers is straightforward: demonstrate consistent performance at state and national championships, earn visibility with selectors, and move into the consideration pool for Indian team places and international programmes.

Lama's progression along that pathway — from domestic training to a World Cup squad to a Commonwealth camp — shows the pipeline functioning as intended. Madhya Pradesh's Sports and Youth Welfare Minister Vishvas Kailash Sarang publicly congratulated both MP boxers selected for the Glasgow camp, noting that the opportunity would play an important role in each athlete's sporting development.

The Glasgow 2026 Commonwealth Games begin on 23 July, bringing together athletes from across the Commonwealth in ten sports. The Boxing Camp that Lama is attending overlaps with the Games calendar; the exposure and coaching during those two weeks are directly relevant to the competitive period that follows.

Women's boxing in India has sustained a strong pipeline of talent through state academies, with athletes from cities like Bhopal regularly breaking through to Asian Championships and Commonwealth podiums. What produces those results is exactly the combination Mahi Lama is building: a domestic record strong enough to earn international selections, and the accumulated learning from each one of those programmes abroad.

The two selections in 2026 are already the beginning of a visible international record. What Glasgow gives her next is the most useful kind of preparation: structured international training, a month before the competitive season fully opens.
