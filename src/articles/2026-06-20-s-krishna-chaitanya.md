On June 20, 2026, Madhya Pradesh Chief Minister Dr. Mohan Yadav and Union Housing and Urban Affairs Minister Manohar Lal Khattar stood at Chandrawatiganj in Indore to inaugurate the city's biggest piece of new urban infrastructure in years. The Yellow Line of Indore Metro had just grown from a 6-kilometre pilot into a nearly 17-kilometre network ready to serve the densest parts of the city. Behind the ceremony was S. Krishna Chaitanya — the 2013-batch IAS officer who has run Madhya Pradesh Metro Rail Corporation Limited (MPMRCL) since August 2024 — and nearly two years of relentless inspections, night trials, and contractor coordination.

The Yellow Line's Phase 1, a 6-kilometre stretch from Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Terminal to Veerangana Jhalkari Bai Station in the railway zone, had entered service on May 31, 2025. Useful, but limited to one end of the city. Phase 2 was the hard part — 11 kilometres of elevated track cutting through some of Indore's busiest residential and commercial belts, from Super Corridor-2 all the way to Radisson Square.

## What 11 More Kilometres Mean for Indore

The new corridor passes through Vijay Nagar, Gandhi Nagar, Sukhliya, Scheme-78, Scheme-54, and ISBT — a lineup of areas where hundreds of thousands of people live and work. Metro authorities estimate that **4 to 6 lakh residents** will benefit directly once passenger services begin on June 21. The long-term catchment population along the Super Corridor alone is expected to reach **10 lakh people** as the zone continues to develop.

Chaitanya spent months on the project site. Rail Analysis India's coverage of his inspection visits shows him walking every elevated section, reviewing construction quality station by station, and instructing engineers on safety standards and operational readiness. During one three-day field inspection, he covered the entire priority corridor — not the kind of oversight that happens from an office.

Indore has earned a reputation as one of India's most efficiently managed cities, winning the Swachh Survekshan title seven times in a row. A functioning metro is the infrastructure backbone that a city of this size needs to maintain that quality of life. Chaitanya came to MPMRCL from the Damoh district collectorate and the MP State Employment Council, with both an engineering degree and an MBA. His background — technical enough to read a construction problem, administrative enough to hold a contractor to a deadline — suited the role.

The Yellow Line ultimately runs 29 stations across 33.53 kilometres from the railway station to the airport. Phase 2 is the single biggest advance toward completing it. Passenger service opens on June 21, and Indore begins moving differently.
