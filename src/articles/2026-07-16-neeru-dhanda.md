On the afternoon of July 12, 2026, at the ISSF Shotgun World Cup in Lonato, Italy, 26-year-old Neeru Dhanda stood in the eight-woman final for the women's trap event. No Indian woman had ever won an individual trap gold at an ISSF World Cup. In thirty shots, she changed that.

Dhanda had topped the qualification round with **121 hits out of 125**, a national record that put her ahead of every other shooter in the field. In the final, she held that lead through a tense battle, going **27 out of 30** to beat former world champion Carole Cormenier of France. The win earned her a place in Indian shooting history.

"Medals will now come consistently," she told reporters after the ceremony.

## From Shivpuri to the Lonato Final

Dhanda was born in Jind district, Haryana, but her family relocated to Shivpuri in Madhya Pradesh more than a decade ago. It was watching her cousin Lakshay Sheoran — who won silver at the 2018 Asian Games in trap — that turned a passing interest into serious ambition.

She cleared the selection trials for the **Madhya Pradesh State Shooting Academy in Bhopal**, part of the MP Department of Sports and Youth Welfare's network of high-performance training centres. The structured coaching at Bhopal, combined with access to proper range infrastructure, gave her career its foundation.

When the Indian Army opened rank-and-file recruitment to women, Dhanda enrolled in the Corps of Military Police and eventually became a **Naib Subedar in the Army Marksmanship Unit**. Her current coaching team is a strong one: British Olympic trap gold medallist Peter Wilson coaches her, and Olympian Mansher Singh serves as her High-Performance Manager. The Target Olympic Podium Scheme (TOPS) provides additional backing.

Trap shooting rewards a specific kind of resilience — a shooter must call and break a fast-moving clay target that can fly in any direction, repeating the same decisive movement across 125 qualification attempts without letting errors compound. Dhanda's performance in Lonato, wire to wire, showed she has built exactly that quality.

The victory at the ISSF World Cup puts Dhanda in line for India's shooting squad for the upcoming Commonwealth Games and keeps her well placed on the road to the 2028 Olympics. For Madhya Pradesh, which has steadily built its shooting infrastructure over the past decade, her world cup gold is a concrete return on that long-term investment.
