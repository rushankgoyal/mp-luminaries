On 2 May 2026, at an athletics meet in Bhubaneswar, a 21-year-old from Bhopal ran down the runway, planted his pole and cleared **5.41 metres** on his very first attempt — breaking India's national pole vault record by a centimetre. A few weeks later, he broke it again.

Kuldeep Kumar trains at the TT Nagar athletics facility in Bhopal under coach Ghanshyam Yadav, and he has spent the past three months quietly rewriting what Indian pole vaulters are capable of. On 4 June, the Athletics Federation of India named him to its 54-member squad for the inaugural Asian U23 Athletics Championships, beginning in Ordos, China on 9 July. The competition starts in four days.

## Three Competitions, One Trajectory

The 2026 athletics season gave Kumar three separate stages to make his case.

In April, at the Indian Athletics Series 2 in Jaipur, he and his rival Dev Kumar Meena — another record-holder from the same generation — both cleared **5.25 metres**, the Commonwealth Games qualification standard. Kumar won the event on countback: he had cleared the height earlier in the competition.

Then came the Indian Indoor Open in Bhubaneswar on 2 May. Kumar cleared 5.41 metres on his opening attempt, erasing the national record of 5.40m. The record had belonged to Meena. It was, by athletics convention, a polite but unambiguous statement.

At the Federation Cup shortly after, the two met again. This time both cleared **5.45 metres** — a new national record, jointly held. The gold went to Meena on countback; Kumar took silver. Neither had failed where it mattered. The separation came down to the count of misses at earlier heights. The selectors, clearly, were watching both.

At the 65th National Inter-State Athletics Championships in Bhubaneswar in late June, Kumar cleared 5.20 metres for a bronze medal — a conservative clearance in a championship-conditions meet, but consistent form that rounded out four months of successive podium finishes.

## What This Means for MP Athletics

India's pole vault record hovered in the 5.10–5.20 metre range for years. The arrival of Kumar and Meena — both in their early twenties, both training within the domestic system — has moved that ceiling sharply upward. Both now exceed the **CWG 2026 qualification standard** and have matched the Asian Games qualifying mark.

For Madhya Pradesh, which has long invested in its wrestling, boxing and shooting academies, a world-competitive pole vaulter emerging from TT Nagar in Bhopal represents a different kind of return on infrastructure. Coach Ghanshyam Yadav has now produced two national record holders from the same facility.

In Ordos this week, Kumar carries the record, the form, and the quiet expectation of a state that tends to celebrate its athletes only after the rest of the country notices them first.
