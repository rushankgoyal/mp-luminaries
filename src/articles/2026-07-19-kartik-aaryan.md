When the 72nd National Film Awards were announced on 18 July 2026, Kartik Aaryan watched the broadcast with his parents — his father, Dr. Manish Tiwari, and his mother, Dr. Mala Tiwari — and heard his name called for Best Actor. He broke into a scream. His mother placed a kiss on his cheek. The video of that moment, which he shared on Instagram, went immediately viral: a doctor's son from Gwalior had won India's most prestigious film honour.

Kartik was born on 22 November 1990 in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, and attended St. Paul's School in the city. His parents are both doctors, and the expectation was a career in medicine or engineering. He enrolled in a biotechnology degree at DY Patil University in Navi Mumbai — and quietly prepared for film auditions at the same time. His debut film, Pyaar Ka Punchnama, came in 2011. Over the following fifteen years he built a reputation as a leading man who could carry both comedy and serious drama, before Chandu Champion (2024) gave him the role of a lifetime.

## A Soldier Who Became a Swimmer

Chandu Champion, directed by Kabir Khan, is a biographical sports film about Murlikant Petkar — a man whose story had gone mostly untold for decades. Petkar served in the Indian Army and was wounded **nine times** during the 1965 India-Pakistan war. After his recovery, he turned to swimming and at the 1972 Paralympics in Heidelberg, Germany, he won gold in the **50-metre freestyle** event — the first Paralympic gold medal ever won by an Indian.

To portray Petkar across these different chapters of his life — the young soldier, the recovering patient, the disciplined swimmer — Kartik underwent an intensive physical and emotional preparation. Kabir Khan has said the film demanded Kartik unlearn the polish of a mainstream star and find the rough texture of a real person who survived something extreme. The award jury agreed that he managed that shift.

Kartik shared the Best Actor prize with Malayalam actor Mammootty, who was honoured for Bramayugam.

> "Some moments are simply bigger than words, and this is one of them. A dream I've carried for years has finally come true." — Kartik Aaryan, on Instagram after the announcement

For Madhya Pradesh, the recognition is personal and historical at once. Gwalior has given India great musicians, athletes and scientists, but a National Award for acting is a rare addition to that ledger. For young people in smaller MP cities who carry ambitions that feel too large for where they live, Kartik Aaryan's path — from the Fort city's schools to Bollywood's highest honour — is its own kind of proof.
