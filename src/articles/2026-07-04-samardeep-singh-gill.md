On 25 May 2026, at Birsa Munda Stadium in Ranchi, Samardeep Singh Gill stepped into the shot put circle at the National Senior Athletics Championships and threw the iron ball **20.46 metres** — a personal best, a national-championships gold, and a clear of the **20.36m CWG qualification standard**. Standing across the infield was Tajinderpal Singh Toor, the Asian Games champion and longtime face of Indian shot put. Gill outthrew him. He is now in India's official 32-member athletics squad for the Commonwealth Games 2026 in Glasgow.

Gill is 25, trains at Tatya Tope Stadium in Bhopal under coach K. Sandeep Singh, and represents Madhya Pradesh in every domestic competition. He is not, by birth, an MP boy — he was born in Valsad, Gujarat, where his father Mahender Singh Gill works as a Bridge Railways Inspector. The family has no athletics background. What pulled him toward shot put was an elder brother who took him to a local competition when he was a teenager, where he threw 11 metres on instinct and someone suggested he could be trained. He left Gujarat for Bhopal, joined the MP Academy, and has not looked back.

## From 11 Metres to the Commonwealth Games

The distance between that first casual throw and his Ranchi personal best is just over nine metres — the product of years of technical refinement, weight training, and a string of podium results that had been quietly building Gill's national profile before Ranchi made it undeniable.

At the South Asian Athletics Championships in 2025, he won gold with **19.59m**. At the Khelo India University Games 2025, another gold, this time **19.42m**. Each competition added half a metre or a metre to his marks, a trajectory that made the Ranchi result feel like a natural conclusion rather than a surprise.

His 4th-attempt throw of **20.46m** in Ranchi mattered for two reasons. It beat Tajinderpal Toor — a psychologically significant result against the man who has dominated Indian shot put for the better part of a decade. And it cleared the qualifying mark the Athletics Federation of India had set for CWG selection. The Bridge reported India's complete athletics contingent for Glasgow on 3 July, with Gill confirmed in the squad.

"Hope to make India proud in major competitions," Gill said after the Ranchi win. It is the kind of unadorned statement athletes make when the result speaks first and the words need only follow.

Madhya Pradesh has been producing distance runners, wrestlers and boxers for years, but an international-level shot putter is rarer. Gill's route — from Gujarat to Bhopal, from a casual teenage throw to a national championship — runs through the MP Academy's system. When he competes in Glasgow, he will be doing so as a product of that system, and as proof that the infrastructure being built in Bhopal can produce athletes who belong on Commonwealth Games fields.
