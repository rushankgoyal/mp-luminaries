On 7 July 2026, Sports Minister Vishvas Sarang welcomed 22 athletes at TT Nagar Stadium in Bhopal who had all qualified for the Asian Games in Aichi-Nagoya, Japan. Among them was Shikha Chauhan, a kayaker from the Madhya Pradesh State Water Sports Academy, whose path to this moment had been built one national medal at a time.

Shikha competes in women's kayaking singles slalom — abbreviated K1 in competition sheets. A kayaker in slalom must steer a narrow fibreglass boat through a sequence of hanging gates strung over a stretch of fast-moving whitewater. A missed gate or a clipped pole adds two seconds to the time; in tight finals, that is often the difference between gold and no podium at all. She trains daily on the Lower Lake in Bhopal, where the state's Water Sports Academy has run a structured paddling programme since 2007.

## Gold at Dal Lake, Silver in China

The clearest public measure of where Shikha stands came in August 2025, when the inaugural Khelo India Water Sports Festival was held at Dal Lake in Srinagar — the first national water-sports competition of its kind. Madhya Pradesh came in as the most consistent paddle-sports programme in the country and left with 10 of the 24 gold medals on offer, topping the overall team championship. Shikha won the women's K1 slalom gold, finishing ahead of all competitors from across India.

Before that Dal Lake result, she had been part of the Indian team that won a silver medal at the Asian Canoe Slalom Championship in Guizhou, China — a historic first for the national canoe slalom programme at the continental level. The combination of the Asian medal and her domestic performance made her selection for the 2026 Asian Games squad straightforward.

The Madhya Pradesh Water Sports Academy has produced athletes across two decades of national championships. Its paddlers represent India at events in Asia and beyond, making it the country's most productive single training base for canoe and kayak slalom. Shikha is among the current generation trained under this system who has reached the senior international stage.

## Why the Asian Games Matter Here

Canoe slalom has not traditionally been a sport that draws large crowds or headlines in India. Madhya Pradesh's programme changed that quietly — not through marquee names or prize money, but through sustained training infrastructure at the state level. The Lower Lake in Bhopal gave athletes a reliable venue; the academy gave them coaching, equipment, and a competition calendar.

Each time a paddler from this landlocked state places on a podium — at a national championship, an Asian continental event, or now the Asian Games — it confirms that the investment is working. For Shikha Chauhan, arriving at TT Nagar Stadium on 7 July alongside 21 other qualified athletes, the ceremony marked more than personal recognition. It marked what a state-backed programme can produce when it commits to a generation of young competitors and holds that commitment over years.

The Asian Games 2026 begin in September. Shikha Chauhan will be at the start line.
