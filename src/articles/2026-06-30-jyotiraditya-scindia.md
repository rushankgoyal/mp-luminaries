Union Minister Jyotiraditya Scindia spent four days in his Guna constituency from 27 June 2026, moving from village to village to inaugurate and lay foundation stones for projects totaling **₹350 crore**. Roads, power substations, a solar plant, a river revival scheme, a sports academy, and a new train service — the visit covered the full range of a constituency's practical needs, and Scindia was present for each one.

The centrepiece of the visit was the **Kuno River Revival Project**, launched at **₹43 crore**. Scindia began at the source of the Kuno River, where he planted 700 saplings under Prime Minister Narendra Modi's *Ek Ped Maa Ke Naam* campaign. On the same occasion he launched the **Jal Ganga Sanvardhan Abhiyan** — a people's movement for river protection — and called on citizens to take responsibility for water and forest conservation alongside the government.

> "Earth and nature are the only possible source of human survival."

The Kuno River flows through the Kuno National Park, the forest that became internationally known in 2022 as the site of India's cheetah reintroduction programme. Restoring the health of the river that runs through that landscape has consequences both for the local farming communities that depend on the water and for the wider ecological project in the region.

## What ₹350 Crore Looks Like on the Ground

The visit combined inaugurations of completed projects with foundation-stone ceremonies for work still to come. Scindia commissioned a **4.5 MW solar power plant** at Ruthiyai under the PM-KUSUM scheme at a cost of **₹20 crore**, designed to reduce electricity bills for farmers in the surrounding area. He inaugurated the **Jeeto Badminton Academy** in Guna, giving young athletes in a district with limited sports infrastructure a professional training venue. He also flagged off the new **Bina-Guna MEMU train service** from Guna Railway Station, improving rail access for tens of thousands of commuters across the district.

The largest single project is the upcoming **₹278-crore electricity substation** whose foundation stone was laid during the visit. When commissioned, it will supply power to more than **2,25,000 people** across approximately **225 villages**, with **100 kilometres of new transmission lines** to be installed alongside it. For a part of rural Madhya Pradesh where power cuts have historically been a daily fact of life, reliable grid supply will change the economics of small businesses, irrigation pumps, and schools alike.

Scindia also held public grievance meetings during the visit, providing a direct channel for residents to raise concerns about central and state government services in person.

Guna district sits in northern Madhya Pradesh, a region that has historically lagged behind on infrastructure investment. None of the work announced this week will be felt immediately — the substation alone will take time to build, and the Kuno River will not recover in a single planting season. But in four days, Scindia put **₹350 crore** of projects into motion and made the case that showing up matters as much as sanctioning from a distance.
