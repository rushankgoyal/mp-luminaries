The target was **258 runs**. Chambal Ghariyals' captain Shubham Sharma had just been dismissed for a first-ball duck. The total on the Holkar Stadium scoreboard in Indore on June 12, 2026 was the highest ever set in the Aditya Birla Group **Madhya Pradesh League T20 Scindia Cup 2026** — posted by Gwalior Cheetahs, who had batted first and given their bowlers what looked like a match-winning total. Then Ankush Singh walked to the crease and changed the shape of the chase entirely.

The 27-year-old left-handed batter from Bhopal struck **101 off 33 balls** — eleven sixes and six fours — registering the fastest century in the history of the MPL T20. By the time Chambal Ghariyals completed the chase by three wickets, they had broken two tournament records in the same afternoon: the highest total successfully chased, and the fastest individual hundred the competition had ever seen.

## A Bhopal Batter Built for Big Chases

Ankush Singh was born in Bhopal on **October 12, 1998**, and has been part of the Madhya Pradesh Ranji Trophy setup since his List A debut on October 8, 2018. A left-handed batter who also bowls leg-breaks and googlies, he made his T20 debut for Madhya Pradesh on November 26, 2025 — a relatively recent entry into the format at the state level.

The MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026, in its expanded format of ten men's franchises competing at Holkar Stadium through June, gave him his largest domestic platform. The tournament is designed to surface MP's best T20 talent: franchise structure, full broadcast coverage, and a schedule that runs from June 3 to the final on June 26.

He had already been delivering for Chambal Ghariyals in earlier matches: **42 off 14 balls** in a nine-wicket win over Bundelkhand Bulls, and **58 not out off 36 balls** in another successful chase. But the June 12 innings against Gwalior Cheetahs was something different in circumstance and scale. Facing the highest target in tournament history, with his captain back in the pavilion before he had faced a ball, Ankush attacked from the first delivery. Eleven sixes in an innings of only 33 balls means he was clearing the boundary roughly every third delivery.

His dismissal triggered a brief wobble. Chambal slipped to **155 for 6 in 11.2 overs** before Rohit Kumar Gupta and Aman Bhadoriya steadied the innings and guided the team to a three-wicket win. The result extended the Ghariyals' unbeaten run in the tournament and placed them firmly among the title contenders heading into the knockout stage.

For Madhya Pradesh cricket, a record-breaking century in a record-breaking chase at Holkar Stadium is exactly what a state franchise league is designed to produce: a concrete moment that moves a domestic batter from promising to noticed. The MPL T20 final is on June 26. Ankush Singh's name is in the record book well before it.
