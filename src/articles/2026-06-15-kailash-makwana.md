In June 2026, Kailash Makwana convened a review meeting of senior Madhya Pradesh Police officers and announced the next step in a programme that had already crossed a milestone. 'Nashe Se Doori, Hai Zaroori 2.0' — the second edition of MP Police's state-wide anti-drug awareness campaign — will run from **15 to 31 July 2026**, covering every police station in the state. It follows an original campaign from July 2025 that reached record scale, earned global recognition, and was featured at the 8th World Book of Records Awards Ceremony held at the UK Parliament in London on 13 September 2025.

Makwana is an IPS officer who has led the Madhya Pradesh Police through a period of significant milestones. In December 2025, the last remaining Naxalite cadres in Balaghat district surrendered, and the state was declared free of Naxalism after four decades. With that goal achieved, Makwana publicly set the next one: a Drug-Free Madhya Pradesh within three years. The anti-drug campaign is how that target begins to be tracked.

## 1,175 Police Stations, 6.35 Crore People

The original 'Nashe Se Doori, Hai Zaroori' ran between **15 and 30 July 2025** — fifteen days, every district, every police station. The mechanics were deliberately simple: uniformed officers, NGO volunteers, teachers, and community groups ran direct awareness sessions in schools, community halls, and neighbourhoods, focusing on young people and the specific consequences of drug and alcohol dependence. The campaign operated out of all **1,175 police stations across 57 districts**. Direct, face-to-face engagement reached approximately **23 lakh people**. The campaign's social media reach extended to **6.35 crore people** — more than 40 per cent of Madhya Pradesh's population.

The World Book of Records recognised the campaign not for its message — anti-drug awareness is common — but for the verified scale and simultaneous delivery: a single, structured, state-wide intervention executed within a defined window across an entire state's police infrastructure. World Book of Records CEO Santosh Shukla presented the certificate at the Madhya Pradesh Police Headquarters in Bhopal. Makwana was subsequently invited to the 8th WBR Awards Ceremony at the UK Parliament in London, where a "Say No to Drugs" oath modelled on the MP Police campaign was among the highlights.

## The Review Meeting and What Comes Next

The June 2026 review covered a range of priorities: crime control metrics, resolution of pending court cases, e-office adoption across the state's police infrastructure, and recruitment progress. The 2.0 campaign sits within this wider plan, not apart from it. Makwana has been clear that the drug problem is not a welfare issue separate from policing — it is intertwined with crime rates, family stability, and youth employment in ways that enforcement alone cannot address. The campaign's community-engagement model is designed to work alongside, not instead of, conventional enforcement.

Madhya Pradesh has now produced a policing programme that was featured at a UK Parliamentary venue alongside achievers from across the world. For a state that is more often discussed for agriculture and pilgrimage tourism than for institutional innovation, the recognition marks a shift in what the rest of the country associates with MP's public administration. The harder test — measurable reduction in drug dependence — will come in the years ahead.
