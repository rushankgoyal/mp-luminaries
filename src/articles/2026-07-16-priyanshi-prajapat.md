Haryana swept all three team titles at the U-23 National Wrestling Championship held in Rohtak in July 2026. But the individual gold in women's freestyle 50 kg went to someone from a different state. Priyanshi Prajapat of Madhya Pradesh outperformed her weight category field to take the national title, the clearest domestic proof yet of what her international results have been signalling for more than a year.

Rohtak is a wrestling city — it produces national champions regularly, and winning there means matching a competitive domestic standard. Priyanshi, shaped at the Madhya Pradesh State Wrestling Academy, did exactly that.

## Built at the MP Academy, Tested Abroad

Priyanshi grew up in Ujjain, one of Madhya Pradesh's most historic cities, and came through the state's wrestling academy system — a network that has sent athletes to national and international competition for over a decade.

Her international record is already serious. In 2025, she won gold at an Asian Series ranking event in Vietnam in the 50 kg category, a result that prompted Chief Minister Mohan Yadav to personally congratulate her. In February 2026, she reached the gold-medal bout of the **Muhamet Malo International Ranking Series** in Tirana, Albania — a prestigious Ranking Series event that draws the world's top-ranked wrestlers — before finishing with a silver medal after a closely contested final.

That run of results meant she arrived at the U-23 Nationals in Rohtak not as an outside bet but as a genuine contender. She lived up to that billing, winning the 50 kg gold and establishing herself as India's leading wrestler in her age group at that weight.

## What the Gold Sets Up

U-23 National Champions are the primary pool from which India selects its squad for the **U-23 World Wrestling Championships** — the next major target for wrestlers in the under-23 bracket. For Priyanshi, who has already tested herself in World Ranking Series competition, that prospect is a realistic one rather than an aspiration.

Madhya Pradesh has invested steadily in wrestling infrastructure. The state wrestling academy has provided training, competitive exposure, and coaching for athletes who have gone on to represent India. Priyanshi's national gold — won in a field that included wrestlers from states with far larger wrestling traditions — shows the academy pipeline is producing results.

For Ujjain, it is a notable moment. The city's contribution to Madhya Pradesh's sporting identity has often come through classical arts and culture. Priyanshi Prajapat is writing a new chapter in a wrestling jersey.
