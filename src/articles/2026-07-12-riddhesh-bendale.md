The 56th International Physics Olympiad closed in Bucaramanga, Colombia, on 12 July 2026 with a result that will be talked about in Indore for years. Riddhesh Anant Bendale, a student at Allen Career Institute in Indore, collected a gold medal — his second at consecutive International Physics Olympiads. A year earlier, at IPhO 2025 in France, he had been one of three Indian gold medalists. This year he was one of five, as part of a historic Indian team that left Colombia without a single silver or bronze: every member returned with gold.

India had never before swept every gold medal available at the International Physics Olympiad. Riddhesh was one of four Allen students selected across India for the 2026 squad, and the only member of the team from Madhya Pradesh. His road to Colombia had already produced headlines earlier in 2026: in JEE Main, he secured **All India Rank 35** with a percentile score of **99.9992186**, recording perfect 100 scores in both Physics and Mathematics — making him Madhya Pradesh's highest-ranked candidate in that examination.

## Five Golds from Five Attempts

The 56th IPhO brought together **381 students from 87 countries** to Bucaramanga. When results were announced, India stood at the joint top of the global rankings alongside China, Kazakhstan, Russia, South Korea, and Taiwan. India's five gold medalists were Riddhesh Anant Bendale from Indore, Kanishk Jain from Pune, Rishit Garg from Delhi, Shresth Suraiya from Mumbai, and Svarit Joshi from Ahmedabad. The country already held a remarkable IPhO record — historically, around 42 per cent of all Indian participants have won gold. In 2026, the entire team converted.

Winning consecutive IPhO gold medals places Riddhesh in a very small group. The olympiad is open only to students who have not yet started university, meaning every participant is drawn from the absolute peak of pre-university physics talent in their country. Performing at the highest level once requires exceptional preparation; repeating it the following year shows something more durable.

The headline for Madhya Pradesh is simple: a student from Indore just helped India finish joint first in the world in physics. In a city that has long produced strong JEE results and science talent, Riddhesh Bendale's back-to-back gold medals show that the aspiration reaches beyond entrance exams to the very top of the global stage.
