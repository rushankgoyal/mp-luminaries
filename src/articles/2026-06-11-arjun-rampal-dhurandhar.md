Indian cinema loves a debut. It is far stingier with second acts — which is what makes Arjun Rampal's current run so satisfying to watch.

The actor, born in **Jabalpur in 1972**, is in the middle of the biggest stretch of his three-decade career. His portrayal of **Major Iqbal** in *Dhurandhar* (2025) and its sequel *Dhurandhar: The Revenge* (2026) has been showered with praise — and the two films have done something almost no pair of films ever does: they now sit as the **second and fifth highest grossing Indian films of all time**.

## The long road to Major Iqbal

Rampal's career has never followed the standard Bollywood script. He arrived as a model-turned-leading-man, weathered years of being underestimated, and then methodically proved the doubters wrong — most famously with a National Film Award for *Rock On!!* in 2008. But character work is where he has always been most dangerous, and Major Iqbal — a chilling figure drawn from the real-world militant Ilyas Kashmiri — gave him the richest canvas of his career. Critics and audiences agreed: the menace, restraint and screen weight he brought to the role became one of the franchise's defining elements.

There is something fitting about a Jabalpur-born actor anchoring a national phenomenon. Madhya Pradesh has sent generations of performers to Mumbai — Ashutosh Rana, Raghubir Yadav, Annu Kapoor, Kartik Aaryan among them — yet the state rarely gets counted among India's cinema heartlands. Every time one of its own owns the country's biggest screens, that ledger gets corrected a little.

At 53, Rampal is delivering the kind of work actors half his age would trade their followings for. The lesson of his second act is simple and worth celebrating: in craft, as in much else, patience is a superpower.
