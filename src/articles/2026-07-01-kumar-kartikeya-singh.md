On a rain-shortened evening at the Holkar Stadium in Indore, with the match cut to 13 overs a side, Kumar Kartikeya Singh produced the most destructive bowling performance of the 2026 MPL T20 Scindia Cup: **6 wickets for 24 runs from three overs**. Royal Nimar Eagles bowled Bhopal Leopards out for 107 and knocked off the target in 9.2 overs to win by eight wickets.

Three of those wickets came on consecutive deliveries. Kartikeya had Pawan Nirwani stumped for 5, clean-bowled Pranjul Puri with the very next ball, then trapped Anurag Malviya lbw to complete a hattrick — one wicket each by stumping, bowled and lbw. It was the third hattrick of MPL Season 3 and arrived in the most complete spell any bowler had produced all tournament.

The performance reinforced something that franchise cricket has been noticing for several years: this left-arm spinner, who has never headlined a franchise press conference, delivers when the conditions are right.

## The Factory Shifts That Came Before the Wickets

Kumar Kartikeya Singh was born in Kuwasi, a village near Sultanpur in Uttar Pradesh, and represents Madhya Pradesh in domestic cricket. He left home at fifteen for Delhi. Before he found steady coaching, he worked night shifts in a factory near Ghaziabad to pay his training fees — clocking out in the early hours, then heading directly to practice. Coach Sanjay Bhardwaj spotted his potential and provided free lodging and mentoring for six years, turning raw ability into a disciplined, attacking game plan.

Kartikeya debuted for Madhya Pradesh in the 2018-19 Vijay Hazare Trophy and came into his own in red-ball cricket. In the **2021-22 Ranji Trophy**, he took **32 wickets at an average of 21**, delivering match-winning spells in the knockout rounds as Madhya Pradesh won their maiden national title — a six-wicket win over Mumbai in the final.

In April 2022, Mumbai Indians signed him for **₹20 lakh** as an injury replacement in the IPL, having never seen him play at that level. He took five wickets in four games. The franchise released him ahead of the 2025 auction and Rajasthan Royals bought him for **₹30 lakh**. He plays for Rajasthan Royals in IPL 2026.

The MPL T20 Scindia Cup is Madhya Pradesh's franchise T20 competition; Season 3 ran from 3 to 26 June 2026 across ten men's franchises. Kartikeya played for Royal Nimar Eagles and produced the tournament's most talked-about bowling performance on 22 June.

At 28, he plays a style of cricket that does not easily generate highlights: patient, accurate, waiting for the error. On that evening in Indore, it generated six dismissals and a hattrick instead — and told, briefly, the story of what the years before those three consecutive balls actually looked like.
