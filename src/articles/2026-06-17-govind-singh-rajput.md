When the Madhya Pradesh government closed its 2026 wheat procurement season in late May, the final number confirmed what officials had been tracking for weeks: **104.22 lakh metric tonnes** of wheat had been purchased from farmers at the minimum support price — the highest in the state in the past ten years, not counting the Covid-disrupted period. More than **13.41 lakh farmers** sold their crop to government agencies, making it the highest farmer participation in any single state in the country this procurement season. The minister responsible was Govind Singh Rajput, who holds the Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Protection portfolio in the cabinet of Chief Minister Mohan Yadav.

Rajput was born on August 22, 1963, in Sagar district. He studied politics at Dr. Harisingh Gaur University in Sagar, followed by an MBA, and has built his political career in the Surkhi constituency of Sagar district. He joined the Mohan Yadav cabinet when it was formed in December 2023, taking charge of a ministry whose core task during the rabi marketing season is one of the most logistically demanding in state government: getting hundreds of procurement centres running simultaneously, ensuring payments reach farmers on time, and resolving the dozens of operational problems — shortages of bags, transport gaps, technical snags — that arise during a season when millions of quintals move through the system each day.

## What the Numbers Mean for Farmers

In 2026, Madhya Pradesh declared its year-long focus on farmers as **Kisan Kalyan Varsh** — a year-of-agriculture initiative centred on raising incomes, expanding irrigation, and improving the terms on which farmers sell their crop. The wheat procurement season, which runs from March through May, is the most direct measure of whether the state's farmer-support machinery works in practice.

The government set an initial procurement target of **78 lakh metric tonnes**. As the season progressed and farmer turnout remained strong, the Central government revised the allocation upward to **100 lakh metric tonnes**. The final figure — 104.22 lakh MT — exceeded even that revised target. Of that total, **32.14 lakh metric tonnes** were procured from small and marginal farmers, roughly 8 lakh households, at the rate of **₹2,625 per quintal** — the ₹2,585 national minimum support price plus a ₹40 state bonus. Total payments to farmers from the season reached **₹23,708 crore**.

Madhya Pradesh ranked second nationally, after Punjab, in total wheat procured at MSP. Business Standard noted that the 13.41 lakh farmer count was the highest in the country this season — meaning that despite Punjab procuring more overall volume, no other state's procurement machinery reached more individual farming households than MP's this year.

For Rajput, the season's close represents the most visible result of a year spent monitoring procurements daily, convening committee meetings, and managing the on-ground logistics of a programme that touches more than a million farm families. Kisan Kalyan Varsh has several components — agri-tech, crop insurance, irrigation — but the wheat MSP programme is its most direct cash transfer. More than ₹23,000 crore was placed in farmers' accounts across MP in a single season, not as a subsidy routed through intermediaries but as payment for a crop. The record turnout — 13.41 lakh farmers, up from previous years — suggests the system earned enough trust that more households chose to use it.
