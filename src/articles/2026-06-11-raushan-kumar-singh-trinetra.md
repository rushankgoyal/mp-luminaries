On 5 June 2026, Ujjain Collector Raushan Kumar Singh announced that TRINETRA — the AI surveillance system deployed at the Mahakaleshwar Temple complex — had been selected for the National e-Governance Award 2026. The award will be presented at the 29th National e-Governance Conference, and the system has been described as a national model for technology-driven management at religious sites.

TRINETRA is a network of more than **500 AI cameras** covering the Mahakal Rudrasagar Integrated Development Area — the large redevelopment zone surrounding one of India's twelve Jyotirlingas. The cameras do not simply record: they run facial recognition, automatic number-plate recognition for vehicles entering the complex, real-time video analytics, and crowd density mapping. Together, they give the district administration round-the-clock visibility across every section of a site that draws millions of devotees each year. Gold-category winners at the National e-Governance Awards receive a prize of **₹10 lakh**.

Raushan Kumar Singh is a 2015-batch IAS officer of the Madhya Pradesh cadre. He took charge of Ujjain district on 15 April 2025, arriving after postings as Chief Executive Officer of Morena Zila Panchayat, Commissioner of the Ujjain Municipal Corporation, and Collector of Vidisha. On the day he assumed the Ujjain role, he visited the Mahakaleshwar Temple before entering the collectorate — seeking the deity's permission before taking official charge. It is a customary gesture for many who serve in the city, but it also captures how the collectorate and the temple complex are intertwined here in a way they are not in most Indian districts.

Alongside the collectorate, he chairs Ujjain Smart City Limited, which has pursued a range of technology-led infrastructure projects in the city. TRINETRA is the most nationally visible of those so far.

## Managing Millions of Pilgrims

Mahakaleshwar Temple is among India's most visited pilgrimage sites. It draws large crowds for Bhasma Aarti each morning and far larger surges during Maha Shivaratri, Shravan month, and other religious occasions. Managing crowd flow across multiple entry points, preventing dangerous congestion, tracking missing persons, and monitoring vehicle movement at a site this dense requires the kind of all-area visibility that human deployment alone cannot reliably provide.

TRINETRA was designed to fill that gap. The system integrates its camera network with centralised analytics that can detect crowd surges in real time and allow the administration to redirect pilgrims before a situation becomes dangerous. Vehicle ANPR covers entry and exit routes, helping manage the considerable traffic that a temple of this scale generates throughout the day and especially during peak hours.

TRINETRA is one of **17 projects** selected nationwide for the National Awards for e-Governance 2026, which recognise innovative use of technology to improve public service delivery across central and state government bodies. Its selection places Ujjain alongside project teams from across India that are using digital tools to solve concrete governance problems.

The award matters for Madhya Pradesh beyond the recognition itself. It demonstrates that one of the state's oldest and most culturally significant cities is not simply managing its heritage but actively building infrastructure to do that managing better — and that the template now being tested in Ujjain is one the rest of India can learn from.
