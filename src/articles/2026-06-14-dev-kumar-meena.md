At the National Senior Athletics Federation Cup in Ranchi in late May 2026, Dev Kumar Meena ran down the track for the last time that evening, planted his pole, and cleared **5.45 metres** — a new Indian national record in men's pole vault, held jointly with his training partner Kuldeep Kumar. He won the gold medal on countback. He was 21 years old and six years removed from a district track in Dewas where someone first noticed he could run fast.

Meena is from a farming family in Dewas, Madhya Pradesh, a city of around 375,000 people roughly 35 kilometres east of Indore. He did not begin as a pole vaulter. In November 2020, a Madhya Pradesh government talent-search team spotted him competing in the 400 metres and brought him into the state athletics programme. His coach, Ghanshyam Yadav, watched his speed and build and made a decision: switch to the pole vault. It was, as the results have confirmed, the right call.

By the 2025 National Games in Uttarakhand, Meena had cleared **5.40 metres** to win gold and set a national record at just 19. Shortly after, Cuban coach Ángel García joined his training setup at the Madhya Pradesh Athletics Academy in Bhopal, adding international technical instruction to the foundational work Yadav had built.

## Metres Gained, One Competition at a Time

The 2026 season unfolded like a series of steps. At the National Open Jump competition in Bengaluru in March, Meena cleared **5.25 metres** — the Athletics Federation of India's qualifying standard for the Commonwealth Games. That secured his eligibility. Then came Ranchi.

At the Federation Cup, the national record changed hands within a single evening's competition. Meena cleared 5.42 metres, improving on Kuldeep Kumar's record of 5.41 metres set earlier in the year. Kumar responded by reaching 5.45 metres. Meena then matched it. Both athletes finished as joint national record holders. The **5.45-metre mark** also matches the AFI's qualifying standard for the 2026 Asian Games, making both men eligible for India's squad across two major international competitions.

On 31 May, Prime Minister Narendra Modi named Meena in his Mann Ki Baat address, specifically praising the Federation Cup record alongside four other national marks broken at the same competition. For a young athlete training at a state facility in Bhopal, the recognition reached the highest level in the country.

Pole vault does not often surface in the national sporting conversation in India. The 5.45-metre joint national record changes that slightly — it places Meena and Kumar closer to the Asian elite, and it sets a clear line of progress toward the 2028 Olympics qualification pathway. Meena, from a village in Dewas, discovered sprinting at 15 and pole vault at 16, reached a national record by 19, and now holds the Indian mark at 21.

Madhya Pradesh's athletics system — the state talent searches, the Bhopal academy, coaches like Ghanshyam Yadav — identified him and built the environment around him. He has carried that investment upward, one cleared bar at a time.
