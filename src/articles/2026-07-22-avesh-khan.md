When Chambal Ghariyals completed an unbeaten run through the MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026 and defeated Royal Nimar Eagles by 31 runs in the final, Avesh Khan had taken 13 wickets across their campaign — a contribution that placed the Madhya Pradesh fast bowler at the centre of one of the competition's most dominant title runs. His side went through the entire tournament without dropping a match, and the trophy that followed came with a clear record of who had made it happen.

The result came at a particular moment for Avesh. His stint with Lucknow Super Giants in the 2026 Indian Premier League had been limited — he played few matches and found none of the rhythm that had characterised his better IPL seasons. The Chambal squad, by contrast, gave him consecutive matches, a defined role at the front of the attack, and the kind of form that only builds through regular competitive bowling.

"The team is very balanced, with many talented players," he said before the semi-final, giving the kind of measured assessment that a senior figure offers when the squad's depth matters as much as any single performance. The group proved it. Tripuresh Singh led all Chambal bowlers with 15 wickets; Avesh's 13 placed him close behind as the attack's two driving forces made the difference across the bracket.

## A Return Path through the Red Ball

Avesh Khan is 29. He has played eight ODIs and 25 T20 internationals for India and last wore the blue jersey in November 2024. In the period since, injuries and inconsistency have kept him out of national selection conversations.

The MPL performance has reopened them. Reporting in July 2026 that had catalogued Avesh as a "forgotten pacer" has reframed the question: the body is healthy, the pace is back, and the focus has shifted to the Ranji Trophy. He has set himself a pre-season conditioning target of around 30 overs a week for four weeks so that when the first-class season begins, his body is ready to carry the workload of the longer format through a full campaign.

The Ranji Trophy is the mechanism. Avesh understands that national selectors respond to sustained domestic performances in the red-ball game, and a full season — healthy, consistently taking wickets in Madhya Pradesh's colours — is the most direct argument he can make for a recall.

Madhya Pradesh's domestic structure has been among the country's stronger state setups for over a generation. The squad the state enters in the Ranji Trophy regularly competes at the front of group standings, and it has produced pace bowlers at a rate disproportionate to the state's size. That system gave Avesh the foundation when his career began; returning to it now, after a tournament won and a target set, is the kind of renewal the state programme was built to support.

The 13 wickets are a starting point. The Ranji season will be the test.
