The scoreboard at Holkar Stadium, Indore read **170 for seven** in the 18th over — Chambal Ghariyals still batting in their MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026 semifinal against Jabalpur Royal Lions, two overs remaining. Posting more than 200 felt uncertain. Then Aman Bhadoriya walked to the crease.

The 24-year-old from Gwalior, a slow left-arm bowling all-rounder who had already contributed with the ball, shifted gear completely: **30 runs off 14 deliveries**, boundary after boundary in the final two overs, pushing the team's total well beyond 200. Chambal Ghariyals won by **11 runs**. The final was theirs.

Bhadoriya was born on 15 October 2001 in Gwalior — a city with a long cricket tradition in Madhya Pradesh. His cricketing identity is built around two complementary skills: slow left-arm spin that builds pressure between wickets, and a lower-order batting style that can switch into attack when a match demands it. He made his List A debut in December 2021 against Maharashtra and his T20 debut in October 2022 against Rajasthan, earning a regular place in the Madhya Pradesh state setup across formats.

In the semifinal, both skills were on display. With the ball, he delivered **1 for 22 from four overs** — tidy figures in a 20-over format when the opposition is looking to break free. With the bat, when his team was running out of time, he hit with the flat authority of someone who had practised exactly this situation.

## A Tournament Run Without a Single Loss

Chambal Ghariyals finished the MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026 unbeaten. From the league stage through to the final, they did not lose a single match. At Holkar Stadium on 27 June, they defeated Royal Nimar Eagles by **31 runs** to lift the title, with captain Shubham Sharma's 57 anchoring their batting total. The win completed what was described as a historic double — the Chambal Ghariyals women's team had also won the women's edition without a defeat.

The MPL Scindia Cup is not a casual tournament. Most franchises field Ranji Trophy-contracted MP players alongside emerging talent, and the competition level is taken seriously by state and national selectors. Performances across its matches feed into India A and senior-squad deliberations. A crucial cameo in a semifinal, delivered under pressure when the team needed it most, is precisely the kind of moment that stays in a selector's file.

Bhadoriya has the dual-format value that opens doors: a left-arm spinner who does not concede cheap runs, and a bat that can rescue a difficult middle-overs position or push a total in the final overs. Both were proven in Indore over the course of the 2026 Scindia Cup.

Madhya Pradesh cricket has a habit of producing players who contribute quietly and decisively when it matters. Aman Bhadoriya, 24, from Gwalior, has added his name to that company.
