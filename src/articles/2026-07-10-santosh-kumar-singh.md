On 8 July 2026, Madhya Pradesh Director General of Police Kailash Makwana presented Indore Police Commissioner Santosh Kumar Singh with a shield and a certificate of appreciation at a ceremony in Bhopal. The award recognised Safe Click 2.0 — a 15-day cyber-safety campaign that touched nearly every neighbourhood in the city.

Safe Click 2.0 ran from 24 June to 8 July. Commissioner Singh's teams spread across schools, colleges, banks, hospitals, markets, malls, religious processions, bus stands and railway stations. In those 15 days, Indore Police conducted more than **307 awareness programmes** and held more than 275 offline events that reached over **5 lakh citizens** face to face. The message — "Sochein, Samjhein, Click Karein" (Think, Understand, Click) — travelled through street plays, rallies, marathons, cyber hackathons and LED video displays in public spaces. On social media, the campaign reached more than **75 lakh people**, bringing the total audience above **80 lakh**.

Commissioner Singh dedicated the honour to the city's entire police force and to the citizens of Indore, saying the results reflected their collective effort to stand together against cyber fraud.

## Building Technology While Running the Campaign

Santosh Kumar Singh, a 2000-batch IPS officer recently promoted to the rank of Additional Director General (ADG), has made cybersecurity a defining priority since becoming Indore Police Commissioner in October 2024. Under his watch, the Indore Commissionerate became the first police unit in Madhya Pradesh to develop and deploy its own AI-enabled chatbot — named 'Safe Clicks' — which the Chief Minister officially launched. The chatbot guides citizens through questions about suspicious links, online fraud and digital safety best practices, extending the campaign's reach well beyond individual events.

Safe Click 2.0 was the public-facing arm of a broader investment: training, technology and direct community engagement working in combination rather than separately. A cyber hackathon for students, coordination meetings with bank nodal officers and a dedicated social-media influencer partnership ran alongside the street-level events, each channel reinforcing the others.

Cyber fraud has climbed steadily in Indian cities, and public education rarely works unless it finds people where they actually are — at the auto-stand and the masjid, not just in auditoriums. Indore's Safe Click 2.0 model, built on the ground by Santosh Kumar Singh's team and now recognised at the state level, is one that police commissionerates elsewhere in Madhya Pradesh are looking at closely.
