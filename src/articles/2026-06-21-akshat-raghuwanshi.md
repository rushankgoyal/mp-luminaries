The first time Akshat Raghuwanshi walked out to bat for Madhya Pradesh in a first-class match, he scored a century. He was 18 years old, playing against Meghalaya in the 2021–22 Ranji Trophy. That announcement—a century on debut—turned out to be accurate about who he was. Madhya Pradesh went on to win the Ranji Trophy that season for the first time in the team's history, and Raghuwanshi was in the squad.

Four years on, the 22-year-old right-handed opener is the **highest run-scorer** for the **Rewa Jaguars** in the **MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026**, with **226 runs** through the league stage.

The numbers from specific matches say what season totals alone can obscure. On **8 June**, against Bhopal Leopards, he scored **85 off 32 deliveries**—nine sixes and six fours—in an innings that pushed Rewa past 200 before Bhopal edged a last-over thriller. On **15 June**, against Gwalior Cheetahs, he followed up with **60 off 33 balls** as Rewa posted **238/6**, setting up a 23-run win sealed by Naveen Chouhan's hattrick. Earlier in the tournament, facing Bundelkhand Bulls, Raghuwanshi hit **42 off 14 balls** as Rewa completed their chase in just 5.1 overs.

These are not innings built on one or two lucky hits. The strike rate across these knocks runs well above 200 in each case, and in each game it was the opener's contribution that determined what totals and chases were possible for his side.

## From Ranji Gold to an IPL Contract

Raghuwanshi's path to this season required patience after the early promise. The first-class debut century and the Ranji Trophy medal in 2022 were followed by continued domestic work in the Syed Mushtaq Ali T20 Trophy and the Vijay Hazare Trophy. He captained Madhya Pradesh in the Under-19 Vinoo Mankad Trophy, taking on the added responsibility of leading younger players through a competitive multi-team format.

The turning point came at the **2026 IPL auction**, when the **Lucknow Super Giants bought him for ₹2.20 crore**. He made his IPL debut on **4 May 2026**, walking out at Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai against the home side. The step up in quality and occasion was sharp, and his numbers in the IPL were modest, but the fact of being there—bought by a franchise for real money, selected to open or bat for a leading side—changed the register in which his career is discussed.

The MPL T20 Scindia Cup, played across Madhya Pradesh's grounds with crowds that follow domestic cricket closely, is the stage on which Raghuwanshi is narrowing the gap between the player he has been described as and the player he is demonstrating himself to be. At 22, with a Ranji championship medal, an IPL debut, and 226 MPL runs already this season, the case he is making is a straightforward one.
