When the Airports Authority of India published its 2026 National Customer Satisfaction Index this week, the airport that came in first was not a metro hub or an international gateway. It was **Khajuraho Airport** in Madhya Pradesh — a modest regional terminal that serves one of India's most visited UNESCO World Heritage sites, and that under Airport Director **Santosh Singh** has quietly built a reputation for doing the fundamentals well.

The index evaluated **63 AAI-operated airports** across the country. Passenger feedback measured performance across ground transportation, check-in procedures, security screening, terminal cleanliness, staff behaviour, waiting times, food and beverage services, information systems, and overall experience. Khajuraho Airport cleared every one of those categories well enough to reach the top of the national ranking. Bhopal's **Raja Bhoj Airport placed third**, making Madhya Pradesh the only state with two airports in the top three.

Santosh Singh credited the result to collective effort. "This recognition will provide a major boost to tourism in the region," he said. "Improved air connectivity and high-quality facilities for domestic and foreign tourists visiting the world-famous temples are expected to give new impetus to tourism." He attributed the ranking to the entire airport team, the trust passengers had placed in the institution, and a consistent commitment to service quality.

## A Gateway to Madhya Pradesh's Heritage Crown

Khajuraho Airport sits a few kilometres from the historic temple complex built by the Chandela dynasty between the 9th and 11th centuries. The temples are on the UNESCO World Heritage List and drew more than **4.5 lakh visitors in 2024**, including **33,131 international tourists** — the highest foreign-tourist count among all heritage sites in Madhya Pradesh. The airport is the most direct way in and out for most travellers; the quality of that first and last impression shapes how the state is remembered.

The airport is part of the UDAN regional connectivity scheme, which added a Rewa–Khajuraho service in late 2024, making the temples reachable by air from more corners of Madhya Pradesh itself. The state government has committed more than **₹5,000 crore** to tourism and cultural infrastructure in its 2025–26 budget, positioning Khajuraho as a centrepiece of that spending.

For most passengers, a great airport is one where no single thing goes wrong — where the floor is clean, the staff actually help, security moves without drama, and the wait is bearable. In that sense, Santosh Singh's success at Khajuraho is a reminder that the state's smallest-footprint airports can, with focused management, outperform terminals that carry twenty times the traffic. For MPs that draw visitors from around the world, the gate to the heritage site matters as much as the site itself.
