On the afternoon of 5 July 2026, a bhoomi puja ceremony on the outskirts of Shivpuri changed the economic prospects of a region that has long sat outside Madhya Pradesh's main industrial belt. **Jyotiraditya Scindia**, Union Minister for Communications and the Development of North Eastern Region and Member of Parliament from Guna, joined Chief Minister Dr. Mohan Yadav to lay the foundation stone for Adani Defence & Aerospace's new **₹2,500-crore missile manufacturing complex** — described by its developer as South Asia's largest private-sector missile ecosystem.

Shivpuri falls within the Guna Lok Sabha constituency that Scindia represents. That is not incidental to the story. Bringing a defence corridor investment of this size to the Shivpuri-Guna belt — rather than to an established industrial zone near Indore or Bhopal — was a deliberate priority. Scindia said as much at the ceremony:

> "This landmark project marks the beginning of a new industrial chapter for the Shivpuri-Guna region. By bringing advanced defence manufacturing to the heart of our state, the facility will create high-quality employment opportunities and build a strong industrial ecosystem for the future."

## What Will Be Built

The Shivpuri complex fills a specific and long-standing gap in India's defence supply chain. It will be the country's first backward-integrated private defence manufacturing facility — meaning missile system integration, composite propellant production, and Trinitrotoluene (TNT) manufacturing will all operate within the same campus. Until now, those three functions have been split across different facilities in different states, creating logistical bottlenecks that slow serial production.

The missile programmes this facility will serve are active DRDO-developed systems approaching or in serial production: the **Next Generation Anti-Radiation Missile (NGARM)**, the **RUDRAM-II**, the **Naval Anti-Ship Missile – Short Range (NASM-SR)**, the **Long-Range Glide Bomb 'Gaurav'**, and the **Tactical Autonomous Reconnaissance and Attack (TARA)** system. For the Indian Armed Forces, having a single integrated source for these weapons — rather than assembling components from across the country — significantly reduces production timelines.

The project is projected to generate **5,000 direct and indirect skilled jobs** and create a network of supplier opportunities for micro, small and medium enterprises in the district. For a region without a major manufacturing presence, that kind of employment pipeline matters across a generation, not just a budget cycle.

For Madhya Pradesh, the Shivpuri facility marks the state's entry into strategic defence manufacturing. The Defence Corridor that the state has been developing will have its first large-scale anchor project. For Scindia, it represents a delivery on a long-term commitment to the people of Guna-Shivpuri — not a ribbon cut on someone else's project, but durable industrial infrastructure in the exact region he was elected to represent.
