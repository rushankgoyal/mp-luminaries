On the evening of 10 June at Holkar Stadium in Indore, Ujjain Falcons were in difficulty against defending champions Bhopal Leopards in the Madhya Pradesh Premier League. Madhav Tiwari walked to the wicket and transformed the match. He struck **61 not out off 28 balls**, hitting four sixes, and then followed it with **2 wickets for 26 runs** across four overs. Ujjain won by 16 runs. It was not the first time this tournament that he had been the difference.

Tiwari comes from Mauganj village in Madhya Pradesh's Rewa district. His father is a civil servant; his mother is a teacher. He started playing cricket at eight and worked his way through the MP state system at Amay Khurasiya's Cricket Academy, representing Madhya Pradesh at under-19 and under-23 levels before appearing in the CK Nayudu Trophy for the senior side. At the 2025 IPL auction, Delhi Capitals signed him for ₹40 lakh — a modest price for a player who was already showing two distinct skills in a sport that prizes exactly that combination.

In IPL 2026, he made the most of limited opportunities with DC. Against Punjab Kings, he dismissed both Priyansh Arya and Cooper Connolly in the bowling innings, then came in late in the chase and hit 18 off 8 balls to guide Delhi home. He won the Player of the Match award. The method was the same one he brought to Holkar Stadium a few weeks later: bowling that asks questions, batting that settles them.

## From Rewa to the Orange Cap

The MPL T20 Scindia Cup 2026 is the biggest edition of the tournament yet — ten men's franchises, **44 matches**, three weeks across Holkar Stadium and Daly College Ground in Indore. For Ujjain Falcons, playing in their debut season, Tiwari has been the anchor.

In the tournament opener on 4 June, he struck **62 not out off 31 balls** — five sixes and four boundaries — and picked up two wickets as Ujjain beat Gwalior Cheetahs by 92 runs. Six days later came the Bhopal Leopards match. Six days after that, the Orange Cap: **194 runs in four matches**, leading all batters in the competition.

For a debut franchise, these results mean Ujjain Falcons have arrived in the tournament as genuine contenders in their first season. Tiwari is also their captain — so the runs and wickets carry extra weight. He is not just contributing performances; he is setting the terms for how his team plays.

The Madhya Pradesh Premier League exists to give the state's domestic talent a competitive platform between national seasons. What Tiwari has done in MPL 2026 is demonstrate that the platform can accelerate careers as well as sustain them. Two months ago he was winning Player of the Match awards in the IPL against Punjab Kings. Now he is driving his home state's franchise to victories using the same method: rapid runs when they are needed, wickets when the opposition threatens to take control.

Mauganj is a small city in eastern Madhya Pradesh. It does not regularly produce cricketers who captain teams in franchise tournaments and lead their batters' standings after the first week. Tiwari, 22, is making it the origin story of someone who might.
